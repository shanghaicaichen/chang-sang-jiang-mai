Renying Qikou Pulse Method

Renying pulse (carotid artery) represents Yang and the exterior; Qikou pulse (radial artery) represents Yin and the interior. By simultaneously palpating and comparing the strength of these two pulses, the method establishes the core basis for six-meridian differentiation.

If the Renying pulse is greater than the Qikou pulse, it indicates exogenous exterior syndromes or Yang-excess heat. If Qikou is greater than Renying, it points to internal injury or Yin-excess cold. This direct approach greatly enhances the objectivity and accuracy of clinical diagnosis.

Ten Pulse Images

To facilitate clinical learning, the Chang Sang Jun Pulse Method simplifies the complex traditional 28 pulses into the 'Ten Pulse Images' with the most clinical significance.

These ten pulses include: Rolling, Throbbing, Turbid, Large, Small, Slippery, Choppy, String-taut, Tense, and Weak pulses. Each pulse image directly corresponds to a specific pathological state and medication direction, making pulse diagnosis clear and practical rather than mysterious.
