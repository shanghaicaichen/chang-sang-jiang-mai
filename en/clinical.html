Precision Medication · Acupuncture · Early Warning

Linking theory with practice, making pulse diagnosis the cornerstone and compass of clinical efficacy.

Precision Medication Guide

Based on the calculated 'Pulse Ratio', it directly guides the choice of prescriptions, avoiding blind medication trials.

Qi Deficiency (< 4): Indicates a state of deficiency and cold. Prescriptions focus on warm tonification, such as Xiao Jianzhong Decoction.
Balanced State (4-5): Indicates relatively balanced Qi and blood or mixed deficiency and excess. Use Xiao Jianzhong Decoction combined with Da Chaihu Decoction based on symptoms.
Rapid Pulse (> 5): Indicates excess Yang and heat. Prescriptions focus on clearing heat, such as Da Chaihu Decoction. Warm tonification is strictly forbidden.

Acupuncture: Six-Meridian Differentiation

Based on the Yin-Yang comparison of the 'Renying Qikou Pulse Method', a unique and highly effective acupoint selection protocol was developed.

When Renying > Qikou (Yang excess, Yin deficiency), acupoints focus on Yin meridians to sedate Yang and tonify Yin. When Qikou > Renying (Yin excess, Yang deficiency), focus is on Yang meridians. Combined with the Ten Pulse Images, it guides doctors to apply needles at specific points (like Taiyuan, Renying), achieving immediate responses.

External Treatment & Early Warning

External Treatment Contraindications: The pulse ratio directly determines if moxibustion is suitable. For 'Rapid Pulse' (>5) patients with excess heat, moxibustion is absolutely forbidden. Conversely, 'Qi Deficiency' (<4) patients are prime candidates.

Early Warning for Severe Diseases: Extremely abnormal pulses (like severe turbid or choppy pulses) often appear before biochemical changes. This method has repeatedly provided early warnings for cardiovascular accidents (e.g., pre-stroke) and severe hyperlipidemia, gaining precious intervention time.
